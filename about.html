About Me

Sarah Schmidt

Sarah received her B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Northeastern University and M.S. in Engineering, Science & Technology Entrepreneurship Excellence (ESTEEM) from the University of Notre Dame.

She has experience in analyzing various Pratt & Whitney engines, has designed probes for particle imaging and analysis at a startup in Berlin, helped develop an underwater autonomous vehicle for storage tank inspections at a startup in Boston, and was a Safety Engineer at Amazon Robotics where she was the Safety Lead for the first Amazon Robotics site to launch in Australia.

While at Northeastern she was a member of the Northeastern American Institute for Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) where she worked with a team to develop a mechanism to de-spin a rocket for AIAA’s Project Kármán, a rocket designed to reach the Von Kármán line, 100 km above the Earth’s surface. She was also a member of the Northeastern Women’s Water Polo Team, played in the Northeastern Pep Band, and studied abroad in Thessaloniki, Greece.

In 2020, Sarah graduated from the University of Notre Dame with a Master of Science as a part of the Engineering, Science & Technology Entrepreneurship Excellence Masters Program (ESTEEM). Her thesis focused on the business and engineering applications of using AI and drones for building facade inspections in collaboration with Underwriters Laboratories (UL).

Sarah is currently a Mechanical Engineer at Wayve, where she focuses on both hardware and software testing for autonomous vehicles. She works cross-functionally across several departments on hardware, electrical, and software testing and manages Wayve’s Hardware in the Loop (HiL) benches from planning and build through day-to-day troubleshooting.

Outside of work Sarah enjoys running, swimming, playing bass clarinet in a community band, tabletop gaming, and 3D printing.
